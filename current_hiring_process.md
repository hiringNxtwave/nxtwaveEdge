Transcript 1: Akanksha & Hemanth - EDGE Landing Page.m4a
This conversation focuses on the internal recruitment workflow, screening strategies, and the transition to a centralized portal.
Initial Workflow and Requirements: The process begins when a company shares its requirements, including skills, CTC, job title, stipend, location, and college or branch criteria
. Among these, four are typically mandatory and two are optional
. Based on these filters, the team rolls out applications and shares candidates with the company, usually at a ratio of five candidates per opening
.
Shortlisting and Ratios: For direct interviews, a 5:1 ratio is used, but if an assessment is involved, the entire pool or a higher ratio (10:1 or 15:1) may be shared depending on the number of applications
.
Data Consolidation: Candidate data is consolidated into a standardized format that includes mobile numbers, college names, graduation details, academic info, assessment reports, and TR1/TR2 interview recordings
. For premium companies, the team also adds detailed evaluation summaries and skill scores
.
Screening Reports: The team uses tools to generate screening reports (often as PDFs) that highlight candidate skills, portfolio projects, and interview links
. These reports focus on matching candidates to job descriptions and can highlight specific achievements, such as a candidate having solved over 500 problems on coding platforms
.
Platform Transition: The team discusses moving away from manual Excel sheets, which can create a poor impression on clients
. Instead, companies will be given a portal login to view candidate boards, download profiles, and schedule interviews directly
.
Interview Management: There are approximately 15 possible interview rounds that a company might define, including assessments and various technical and HR rounds
. The team coordinates interview slots with the company, often involving a manual feedback loop to check student availability
.
Transcript 2: Sharan - EDGE Landing Page.m4a
This transcript details the client-facing challenges of the current process and the vision for a "platformized" solution.
Presentation vs. Implementation: Sharan explains that while virtual dashboards are effective for convincing clients during initial meetings, companies often revert to only checking resumes once data is shared via Excel sheets
. Moving to a platformized portal is intended to ensure clients engage with full evaluation reports
.
Client Requirements and Batches: For clients like Sprint, requirements such as CTC, job type (full-time or internship), and location are gathered to initiate matchmaking
. Interest is collected via forms, and candidates are shared in batches to avoid exhausting the pool and to better understand the interview process
.
Current Communication Gaps: Feedback on candidates (selected or rejected) currently happens largely through informal channels like phone calls and WhatsApp, which is not standardized
. Scheduling interviews involves significant back-and-forth communication to check student and recruiter availability
.
The Platform Vision: The proposed platform will include an admin portal for the internal team and a dedicated login for companies
. It aims to integrate with tools like HubSpot to automatically pull requirements and match candidates
. Companies will be able to view their specific job boards, track candidate statuses in real-time, and manage scheduling through integrated buttons
